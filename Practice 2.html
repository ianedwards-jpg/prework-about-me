Biography

Led Zeppelin are an English rock band formed in 1968 in London, England. Guitarist Jimmy Page and multi-instrumentalist John Paul Jones began collaborating in 1967 and soon decided to form their own group. After inviting Hobbestweedle vocalist Robert Plant and drummer John Bonham, they began appearing live and recorded their first studio album in late 1968. Throughout their 12 year career, they released nine albums, went on 28 tours and transformed rock music as we know it. Led Zeppelin began by experiementing with American Blues rock, drawing inspiration from blues legends such as Muddy Waters and John Lee Hooker.

Soon, they began branching into pop and psychadelic rock, quickly becoming one of the most famous bands in the world. Following the death of John Bonham in 1980, Led Zeppelin broke up and its remaining members embarked on successful solo careers. However, they have continued to collaborate with one another on several projects and remain an active presence in the music community to this day.
